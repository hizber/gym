### 1. Dumbbell Bicep Curl (Supine)
![Exercise GIF](https://cdn.jefit.com/assets/img/exercises/gifs/272.gif)

**Equipment:** Dumbbells, flat bench
**Difficulty:** Beginner 
**Type:** Isolation
**Primary muscles:** Biceps brachii, forearms

#### Step-by-Step Technique:
1. Start by lying on a flat bench with your head at one end and feet planted firmly on the floor
2. Grab a dumbbell in each hand with palms facing in, bring arms down to your sides hanging off the bench
3. Slowly raise your arms up until they are level with your chest
4. Curl the dumbbells while twisting your palms so your forearms touch your biceps
5. Hold this position for a count, isolating the bicep muscle and squeezing
6. Slowly lower your arms back to the starting position
7. This lying position provides unique angle and isolation for bicep development

### 2. Cable Bicep Curl (Close Grip)
![Exercise GIF](https://cdn.jefit.com/assets/img/exercises/gifs/1015.gif)

**Equipment:** Cable machine, straight bar attachment
**Difficulty:** Beginner 
**Type:** Isolation
**Primary muscles:** Biceps brachii

#### Step-by-Step Technique:
1. Stand up straight in front of a low pulley cable machine with straight bar attached
2. Keep your arms rested at your sides and feet flat on the floor
3. Grab the bar in a close underhand grip, then slowly raise it up towards your shoulders
4. Isolate the bicep and hold for a count at the top
5. Return back to the starting position with controlled movement
6. Keep elbows close to your body throughout the exercise
7. Close grip targets the biceps with constant cable tension

**üîó Jefit Reference:** [Cable Bicep Curl (Close Grip)](https://www.jefit.com/exercises/1015/cable-bicep-curl-close-grip)

### 3. Barbell Preacher Curl
![Exercise GIF](https://cdn.jefit.com/assets/img/exercises/gifs/113.gif)

**Equipment:** Barbell, preacher bench
**Difficulty:** Beginner 
**Type:** Isolation
**Primary muscles:** Biceps brachii

#### Step-by-Step Technique:
1. Adjust the seat of a preacher bench so that your arms are level with the top of the bench
2. Grab onto a barbell with an underhand (palms up) grip, arms shoulder-width apart
3. Rest your arms against the bench and extend them fully to start
4. While keeping your arms on the bench, slowly curl the bar up towards your head
5. Isolate the bicep and squeeze the muscles at the top
6. Hold for a count and then return back to the starting position
7. Perform this exercise in a slow manner to attain the best results

## üìã Programming Guidelines

### Beginner Program:
- **Sets:** 3-4
- **Reps:** 12-15
- **Weight:** Light to moderate
- **Rest:** 60-90 seconds between sets
- **Focus:** Perfect form and mind-muscle connection

### Intermediate Program:
- **Sets:** 4-5
- **Reps:** 10-12
- **Weight:** Moderate to heavy
- **Rest:** 90-120 seconds between sets
- **Focus:** Progressive overload and controlled tempo

### Advanced Program:
- **Sets:** 4-6
- **Reps:** 8-12 (varying)
- **Weight:** Heavy with perfect form
- **Rest:** 2-3 minutes between sets
- **Focus:** Advanced techniques (drop sets, supersets)

- **Always warm up** with light weights before heavy sets
- **Keep elbows stationary** - avoid swinging or momentum
- **Control the negative** - lower weights slowly for better muscle activation
