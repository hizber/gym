## Exercise Overview
The seated cable row is a fundamental back-building exercise that provides constant tension and allows for heavy loading. Perfect for developing back thickness, improving posture, and building impressive pulling strength with excellent control and safety.

## **Primary Muscles Targeted:**
- **Latissimus Dorsi** (Primary) - Back width and V-taper development
- **Rhomboids & Middle Trapezius** - Upper back thickness and posture
- **Rear Deltoids** - Posterior shoulder development and balance
- **Biceps & Forearms** - Secondary pulling muscles and grip strength

## **Exercise Variations & Alternatives:**

### 1. **ğŸ”— Cable Seated Row** (Standard Handle)
![Cable Seated Row](https://cdn.jefit.com/assets/img/exercises/gifs/21.gif)

**Equipment:** Cable Row Machine  
**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Type:** Strength | **Primary:** Back, Forearms

**Step-by-Step Technique:**
1. **Machine Setup:** Attach the desired handle to the cable row machine. Adjust the seat and chest pad (if available) so that you can reach the handle comfortably without overextending
2. **Starting Position:** Sit down on the machine with your feet securely placed on the footrests. Your knees should be slightly bent
3. **Grip and Posture:** Grasp the handle with both hands, using an overhand or neutral grip (palms facing each other). Sit with a straight back and slight lean forward from the hips, keeping your chest up and core engaged
4. **Executing the Row:** Pull the handle towards your torso, leading with your elbows. Your elbows should stay close to your body. As you pull, squeeze your shoulder blades together and keep your back straight
5. **End Position:** Pull the handle until it reaches your torso, typically around the lower chest or upper abdomen. Hold this position for a brief moment to maximize the contraction in your back muscles
6. **Return Movement:** Slowly extend your arms and return the handle to the starting position, maintaining control of the weight throughout the movement. Keep your back straight and avoid rounding your shoulders as you return to the start

**Jefit Reference:** [Cable Seated Row](https://www.jefit.com/exercises/21/cable-seated-row)

### 2. **ğŸ‹ï¸ Machine Seated Row** (Fixed Path)
![Machine Seated Row](https://cdn.jefit.com/assets/img/exercises/gifs/700.gif)

**Equipment:** Seated Row Machine  
**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Type:** Strength | **Primary:** Back

**Step-by-Step Technique:**
1. **Machine Position:** Start off sitting on a fixed row machine, grabbing the handles in front of you so that your arms are in a full extension and chest is up against the pad in front of you
2. **Movement Initiation:** Slowly contract your back muscles moving your shoulder blades with your arms and pull back on the machine
3. **Peak Contraction:** Hold as you reach a peak position to maximize muscle engagement and ensure proper form
4. **Return Phase:** Then return back to the starting position in a controlled manner, maintaining tension throughout the movement
5. **Repetition Cycle:** Repeat for as many reps and sets as desired, focusing on smooth, controlled movements and proper muscle activation

**Jefit Reference:** [Machine Seated Row](https://www.jefit.com/exercises/700/machine-seated-row)

### 3. **ğŸ”— Cable Rope Seated Row** (Neutral Grip)
![Cable Rope Seated Row](https://cdn.jefit.com/assets/img/exercises/gifs/1050.gif)

**Equipment:** Cable Machine with Rope Attachment  
**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Type:** Strength | **Primary:** Back, Biceps, Shoulders

**Step-by-Step Technique:**
1. **Setup:** Start by attaching a rope extension to a low pulley cable machine and sitting down in front of it, with your feet firmly on the rests and grabbing the rope with a neutral grip
2. **Pulling Motion:** Slowly pull the weight towards you to your sides, squeezing your back muscles throughout the movement
3. **Peak Hold:** Hold for a count at the fully contracted position to maximize muscle activation and ensure proper form
4. **Controlled Return:** Return to the starting position in a smooth, controlled manner while maintaining tension on the muscles
5. **Repetition:** Repeat for as many reps and sets as desired, focusing on the neutral grip advantage and enhanced muscle engagement

**Jefit Reference:** [Cable Rope Seated Row](https://www.jefit.com/exercises/1050/cable-rope-seated-row)
