## Exercise Overview
The Close-Grip Bench Press is the ultimate triceps mass builder and one of the most effective compound movements for upper body pressing strength. This variation shifts emphasis from the chest to the triceps while still maintaining the powerful compound nature of the bench press. It's an essential exercise for anyone serious about building impressive arm size and pressing power.

- **Anterior Deltoids** - Front shoulders for pressing stability and strength
- **Serratus Anterior** - Shoulder blade stability and control

## Exercise Variations & Alternatives

### 1. **ğŸ‹ï¸ Barbell Bench Press (Close Grip)** (Classic Mass Builder)
![Barbell Bench Press Close Grip](https://cdn.jefit.com/assets/img/exercises/gifs/4.gif)

**Equipment:** Barbell, Bench  
**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Type:** Strength | **Primary:** Triceps, Chest, Shoulders

**Step-by-Step Technique:**
1. Load the barbell with an appropriate amount of weight for your fitness level. Use safety clips to secure the weights
2. Lie down on the flat bench with your feet flat on the ground and your head, shoulders, and buttocks firmly pressed against the bench
3. Grip the barbell with your hands closer than shoulder-width apart, around 6-12 inches (15-30 cm) apart. Ensure your thumbs are wrapped around the bar for safety
4. Unrack the barbell by straightening your arms and moving the barbell over your chest. Your arms should be perpendicular to the floor
5. Keep your wrists straight and directly over your elbows to maintain proper alignment and reduce strain
6. Inhale deeply and lower the barbell slowly and under control to your lower chest or upper abdomen
7. Your elbows should stay close to your body, not flaring out. Lower the bar until it lightly touches your chest or is just above it. Do not bounce the bar off your chest
8. Exhale and press the barbell back up to the starting position by fully extending your arms
9. Focus on engaging your triceps during the lift. Keep your elbows close to your body throughout the movement to maximize tricep activation

**Jefit Reference:** [Barbell Bench Press (Close Grip)](https://www.jefit.com/exercises/4/barbell-bench-press-close-grip)

### 2. **ğŸ”— Cable Tricep Pushdown (Rope)** (Isolation Focus)
![Cable Tricep Pushdown Rope](https://cdn.jefit.com/assets/img/exercises/gifs/159.gif)

**Equipment:** Cable machine with rope attachment  
**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Type:** Strength | **Primary:** Triceps

**Step-by-Step Technique:**
1. Start off standing in front of a cable machine, attaching a rope to the high pulley and grabbing the attachment with an overhand (palms down) grip
2. Keeping your abs drawn in, back straight and elbows in at your sides, push the rope down towards your thighs
3. As you push down towards your thighs, split the rope apart at the bottom and isolate the tricep muscle
4. Hold this position for a count and return back up to the starting position
5. Repeat for as many reps and sets as desired

**Jefit Reference:** [Cable Tricep Pushdown (Rope)](https://www.jefit.com/exercises/159/cable-tricep-pushdown-rope)

### 3. **âš™ï¸ Smith Machine Bench Press (Close Grip)** (Controlled Movement)
![Smith Machine Bench Press Close Grip](https://cdn.jefit.com/assets/img/exercises/gifs/248.gif)

**Equipment:** Smith Machine, Bench  
**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Type:** Strength | **Primary:** Triceps, Chest, Shoulders

**Step-by-Step Technique:**
1. Start off by placing a flat bench in the middle of a smith machine
2. Using a close grip, hands about 6 inches apart, unlatch the bar and lower it towards your chest
3. Pause just before the bar hits your chest and raise the bar by extending your arms to the starting position
4. Repeat for as many reps and sets as possible
5. Refrain from bouncing the bar off of your chest as this is improper form and could lead to injury

**Jefit Reference:** [Smith Machine Bench Press (Close Grip)](https://www.jefit.com/exercises/248/smith-machine-bench-press-close-grip)
