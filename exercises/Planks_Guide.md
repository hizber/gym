### 1. Plank
![Exercise GIF](https://cdn.jefit.com/assets/img/exercises/gifs/631.gif)

**Equipment:** None (bodyweight)
**Difficulty:** Beginner 
**Type:** Isometric/Core stabilization
**Primary muscles:** Abs, back

#### Step-by-Step Technique:
1. Start by kneeling on all fours and aligning both hands right below your shoulders
2. Keep your knees beneath your hips
3. Extend both of your feet out behind you and squeeze on your core muscles
4. Make sure that your body is aligned straight
5. Hold this position for about 30 seconds to a minute
6. Release, return back to the starting position
7. Repeat for as many times as you would like to perform this exercise

## üìã Programming Guidelines

### Beginner Program:
- **Duration:** 15-30 seconds per hold
- **Sets:** 3-4 sets
- **Rest:** 30-60 seconds between sets
- **Progression:** Increase hold time by 5-10 seconds weekly
- **Focus:** Perfect form and building base strength

### Intermediate Program:
- **Duration:** 30-60 seconds per hold
- **Sets:** 4-5 sets
- **Rest:** 60-90 seconds between sets
- **Progression:** Add variations or longer holds
- **Focus:** Advanced variations and longer durations

### Advanced Program:
- **Duration:** 60+ seconds or dynamic variations
- **Sets:** 4-6 sets
- **Rest:** 90-120 seconds between sets
- **Progression:** Weighted planks or complex variations
- **Focus:** Advanced progressions and challenges

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Safety Notes

- **Start gradually** - begin with shorter holds and build up
- **Maintain proper alignment** - don't let hips sag or pike up
- **Breathe normally** - don't hold your breath during holds
- **Listen to your body** - stop if you feel lower back pain
- **Focus on quality over quantity** - perfect form is most important
- **Warm up first** - do light movement before plank holds
- **Progress slowly** - increase difficulty gradually to prevent injury
- **Stop when form breaks down** - better to rest than compromise technique

## üéØ Performance Standards

### Beginner Targets:
- **Goal:** Hold standard plank for 30 seconds with perfect form
- **Progression:** Build to 60-second holds
- **Focus:** Form mastery and basic core strength

### Intermediate Targets:
- **Goal:** Hold plank for 90+ seconds, master side planks
- **Progression:** Add dynamic variations and progressions
- **Focus:** Advanced variations and longer holds

### Advanced Targets:
- **Goal:** 2+ minute holds, weighted planks, complex variations
- **Progression:** Sport-specific or advanced progressions
- **Focus:** Functional strength and specialized applications

## üîÑ Exercise Alternatives

### Easier Alternatives:
- **Knee Plank** - Plank from knees instead of feet
- **Incline Plank** - Hands elevated on bench or wall
- **Modified Side Plank** - Bottom knee down for support
- **Dead Bug** - Supine core stability alternative

### Harder Alternatives:
- **Weighted Plank** - Add weight plate on back
- **Single-Arm Plank** - Remove one point of contact
- **Plank with Arm Reaches** - Dynamic arm movements
- **Plank Jacks** - Jump feet apart and together

### Dynamic Variations:
- **Mountain Climbers** - Alternating knee drives
- **Plank Shoulder Taps** - Touch opposite shoulder
- **Plank Walk-Outs** - Walk hands forward and back
- **Plank Rotations** - Rotate from plank to side plank

### Equipment Variations:
- **Stability Ball Plank** - Feet or forearms on ball
- **TRX Plank** - Feet in suspension trainer
- **BOSU Plank** - Unstable surface challenge
- **Resistance Band Plank** - Add external resistance

### Time-Based Challenges:
- **Plank Pyramid** - Increasing and decreasing holds
- **Tabata Planks** - 20 seconds on, 10 seconds rest
- **Plank Ladder** - 10s, 20s, 30s, etc. progression
- **Max Hold Challenge** - Test maximum hold duration
