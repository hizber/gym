## Exercise Overview
The Barbell Bench Press is universally considered the king of upper body exercises - a fundamental compound movement that builds upper body strength, power, and muscle mass. Essential for any serious strength training program and the gold standard for measuring upper body power.

## Exercise Variations & Alternatives

### 1. **üèãÔ∏è Barbell Bench Press** (The Classic)
![Barbell Bench Press](https://cdn.jefit.com/assets/img/exercises/gifs/2.gif)

**Equipment:** Barbell, flat bench  
**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Type:** Strength | **Primary:** Chest, Triceps, Shoulders

**Step-by-Step Technique:**
1. Load the barbell with appropriate weight for your fitness level and use safety clips to secure the weights
2. Lie down on the flat bench with your feet flat on the ground and your head, shoulders, and buttocks firmly pressed against the bench
3. Grip the barbell with both hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, palms facing forward, thumbs wrapped around the bar
4. Unrack the barbell by straightening your arms and moving the barbell over your chest, arms perpendicular to the floor
5. Inhale deeply and lower the barbell slowly and under control to your mid-chest
6. Your elbows should bend at about a 45-degree angle to your body
7. Lower the bar until it lightly touches your chest or is just above it (do not bounce the bar off your chest)
8. Exhale and press the barbell back up to the starting position by fully extending your arms
9. Focus on squeezing your chest muscles as you lift the weight, keep your wrists straight and elbows slightly tucked

**Jefit Reference:** [Barbell Bench Press](https://www.jefit.com/exercises/2/barbell-bench-press)

### 2. **üî© Barbell Bench Press (Close Grip)** (Tricep Focus)
![Barbell Bench Press Close Grip](https://cdn.jefit.com/assets/img/exercises/gifs/4.gif)

**Equipment:** Barbell, flat bench  
**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Type:** Strength | **Primary:** Triceps, Chest, Shoulders

**Step-by-Step Technique:**
1. Load the barbell with appropriate weight and use safety clips to secure the weights
2. Lie down on the flat bench with your feet flat on the ground and your head, shoulders, and buttocks firmly pressed against the bench
3. Grip the barbell with your hands closer than shoulder-width apart, around 6-12 inches (15-30 cm) apart, thumbs wrapped around the bar for safety
4. Unrack the barbell by straightening your arms and moving the barbell over your chest, arms perpendicular to the floor
5. Keep your wrists straight and directly over your elbows to maintain proper alignment
6. Inhale deeply and lower the barbell slowly and under control to your lower chest or upper abdomen
7. Your elbows should stay close to your body, not flaring out
8. Lower the bar until it lightly touches your chest or is just above it (do not bounce the bar off your chest)
9. Exhale and press the barbell back up to the starting position by fully extending your arms
10. Focus on engaging your triceps during the lift and keep your elbows close to your body throughout the movement

**Jefit Reference:** [Barbell Bench Press (Close Grip)](https://www.jefit.com/exercises/4/barbell-bench-press-close-grip)

### 3. **Dumbbell Bench Press** (Better Range of Motion)
![Dumbbell Bench Press](https://cdn.jefit.com/assets/img/exercises/gifs/26.gif)

**Equipment:** Dumbbells, flat bench  
**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Type:** Strength | **Primary:** Chest, Triceps, Shoulders

**Step-by-Step Technique:**
1. Sit on the edge of the bench with a dumbbell in each hand, resting them on your thighs
2. Carefully lie back on the bench, bringing the dumbbells up to chest level as you do so, feet flat on the floor
3. Position the dumbbells to the sides of your chest with your palms facing forward (pronated grip)
4. Your elbows should be bent at about a 90-degree angle and your upper arms should be parallel to the floor
5. Press the dumbbells upward by extending your arms, bringing the weights directly above your chest
6. Keep the dumbbells steady and your wrists straight
7. At the top of the movement, your arms should be fully extended but not locked, and the dumbbells should be close together without touching
8. Hold the top position for a brief moment to maximize the contraction in your chest muscles
9. Lower the dumbbells slowly back to the starting position, keeping your elbows at about a 45-degree angle to your torso
10. Ensure the weights return to chest level, just to the sides of your chest

**Jefit Reference:** [Dumbbell Bench Press](https://www.jefit.com/exercises/26/dumbbell-bench-press)

### 4. **üìê Barbell Bench Press (Wide Grip)** (Outer Chest Focus)
![Barbell Bench Press Wide Grip](https://cdn.jefit.com/assets/img/exercises/gifs/176.gif)

**Equipment:** Barbell, flat bench  
**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Type:** Strength | **Primary:** Chest, Shoulders, Triceps

**Step-by-Step Technique:**
1. Lie flat on a bench with your feet flat on the floor
2. Begin with your back flat on the bench, grabbing onto the barbell with a wider than shoulder-width grip
3. Lift the bar up and off of the rack, bring it over your chest as your starting position
4. Slowly lower the barbell until it is just above your body and you feel a stretch in your chest muscles
5. Hold for a count then extend your arms upward and raise the bar straight up over your chest
6. Keep your abs drawn in and your back flat on the bench throughout this exercise
7. Refrain from bouncing the barbell off of your chest as this is improper form and can lead to injury
8. Repeat for as many reps and sets as desired

**Jefit Reference:** [Barbell Bench Press (Wide Grip)](https://www.jefit.com/exercises/176/barbell-bench-press-wide-grip)

### 5. **‚ö†Ô∏è Barbell Neck Press** (Advanced Variation)
![Barbell Neck Press](https://cdn.jefit.com/assets/img/exercises/gifs/855.gif)

**Equipment:** Barbell, flat bench  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate | **Type:** Strength | **Primary:** Chest, Shoulders, Triceps

**Step-by-Step Technique:**
1. Start by laying with your back on a flat bench using a medium grip on the barbell above you
2. Slowly lift the bar over your neck with your arms locked then come down until the bar is on your neck
3. Hold this position for a count then return back to the starting position squeezing with your chest as you push up
4. Repeat for as many reps and sets as desired

**‚ö†Ô∏è Safety Note:** This is an advanced exercise that requires extreme caution and proper supervision. Not recommended for beginners.

**Jefit Reference:** [Barbell Neck Press](https://www.jefit.com/exercises/855/barbell-neck-press)

### 6. **ü§≤ Dumbbell Bench Press (Palms In)** (Neutral Grip)
![Dumbbell Bench Press Palms In](https://cdn.jefit.com/assets/img/exercises/gifs/1064.gif)

**Equipment:** Dumbbells, flat bench  
**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Type:** Strength | **Primary:** Chest, Shoulders, Triceps

**Step-by-Step Technique:**
1. Start by laying with your back flat on a bench with your feet rested on the floor and holding a dumbbell in each hand
2. Keep the dumbbells rested at your chest as you get yourself into position, holding onto them with a neutral grip (palms facing each other)
3. Extend your hands above your chest and squeeze so that you feel a stretch in your pecs, then hold for a count
4. Then slowly return the dumbbells at the sides of your chest
5. Repeat for as many reps and sets as desired

**Jefit Reference:** [Dumbbell Bench Press (Palms In)](https://www.jefit.com/exercises/1064/dumbbell-bench-press-palms-in)
